# Expanding Plan

This document tracks how the corpus should widen from a small classic core into
a broader but still recognizable AI-dystopia repository.

## Current Expansion Priorities

1. keep the approved set recognizable and high-signal
2. grow literary coverage alongside film and television
3. separate verified quote capture from quote-site hints and discovery leads
4. use author lanes as a deliberate widening strategy rather than waiting for
   titles to surface randomly

## Why This Lane Matters

- it pushes the project beyond film-only discovery
- it surfaces older and more foundational anti-machine language
- it gives us recognizable anchors that readers and players may already know
- it creates a better path toward strong, reusable quotes from books and stories

Frank Herbert is a good example of the kind of adjacent but important literary
voice this lane should catch: anti-machine civilization, control systems,
thinking-machine taboo, and broader reflections on what happens when humans
outsource cognition.

Quote and transcript sites should continue to be used as hint sources, not only
as final citation surfaces.

These should widen the queue, but stronger quote-index or metadata pages should
still anchor final quote records whenever possible.
