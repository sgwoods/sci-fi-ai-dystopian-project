# Initial Review

## Strongest first-pass approvals

- `R.U.R.` gives the corpus historical depth and the origin point for the robot-uprising tradition.
- `2001: A Space Odyssey` is indispensable because HAL is still the canonical AI refusal voice.
- `I Have No Mouth, and I Must Scream` is the most emotionally extreme anti-human superintelligence quote in the set.
- `Colossus: The Forbin Project` covers cold machine authoritarianism better than almost any other entry.
- `WarGames` adds a cautionary AI-risk line that is still widely reused in policy and safety conversations.
- `The Terminator`, `Terminator 2`, and `The Matrix` provide the most recognizable mainstream film cluster.
- `Ex Machina` modernizes the corpus without feeling ephemeral.

## Borderline or hold-for-now

- `Westworld` fits the theme well, especially for opacity and systems designed by other systems, but the line is more diagnostic than iconic.
- `M3GAN` is excellent for consumer-AI autonomy and likely worth keeping in the larger candidate pool, but it is not yet as canonical as the approved set.

## Suggested next research pass

- Add one or two literary entries from novels rather than film adaptations if we can source concise, well-attributed online quotations.
- Add tags for `tone`, `threat_level`, or `voice_style` if the application will need retrieval by mood.
- Consider a second approved export grouped by era: `1920s`, `1960s-1980s`, `1990s+`.
